# Single-Card Boundary

## 1. 这个概念在回答什么问题？
- What makes one learning card narrow enough to stay coherent while still useful?

## 2. 一句话定义
- A single-card boundary is the rule that one card should answer one dominant question and stop before adjacent questions take over.

## 3. 核心本质
- It compresses a thread to one retrieval unit without flattening the idea into something too vague.

## 4. 为什么重要
- It prevents one card from becoming a mixed concept-method-mechanism bundle.
- It makes graph placement and later upgrades easier.

## 5. 核心逻辑
- Identify the dominant question in the thread.
- Keep only the evidence that serves that question.
- Route leftover material to another card instead of stretching the current one.

## 6. 成立前提
- The card family already has clear type boundaries.
- Creating a second card is cheaper than overloading the first card.

## 7. 失效边界
- It fails when the dominant question is still unresolved.
- It also fails when the user really needs a comparison card rather than a single-node card.

## 8. 易混概念与区别
- It is easy to confuse a single-card boundary with oversimplification; the real target is coherence, not forced thinness.

## 9. 正例
- A thread that mainly defines what a boundary is becomes one concept card, while its trimming procedure becomes a separate method card.

## 10. 反例或误用
- A card that explains what boundary means, why drift happens, how to fix it, and which misconception to avoid is already too wide.

## 11. 常见误解
- One card does not mean one paragraph; it means one dominant question.

## 12. 我的表达
- One card, one real question.

## 13. 待验证问题
- Where should borderline comparison-heavy threads split into two cards instead of one?
